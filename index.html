World Happiness

This is based on World Happiness Report from 2016-2023. It is designed to provide valuable insights in understanding the dynamics of happiness and well-being worldwide.

How does happiness differ among countries, regions and continents?

North America, South America, Oceania and Europe holds high ranking in happiness. European countries dominate the top rankings, with Czechia, Finland and Iceland securing the highest scores. Sub-Saharan African nations, including Congo, Zimbabwe, and Sierra Leone, face significant challenges as they occupy the lower positions in the happiness rankings. This indicates the complex interplay of socio-economic factors in shaping well-being across diverse regions.

These visualisations are based on most recently updated 2023 data. The annotation on the right visualisation represent the country with highest happiness score for each region.

How does happiness change over time?

Globally, the average happiness score has increased from 2016 to 2023 highlighted in red.

In 2016, the average global happiness score was 5.47 and increased to 5.56 in 2023.

Oceania and South America suprisingly experienced a decrease in happiness while all other continents increased aligned with the global trend.

What factors affect happiness around the globe?

GDP per capita, social support, and perceptions of corruption

Countries with high social support has a higher happiness rankings. This is also strongly correlated with higher GDP per capita as this often translates to better infrastructure, healthcare, education and job opportunities. Suprisingly, higher happiness scores coincide with increased perceptions of corruption. This may be because happier countries has heighted expectations for transparent governance.

The happiness scores and rankings in this report are determined using data from Gallup World Poll, where respondents rate their own lives from 0 to 10, which are then weighted. GDP per capita is in $USD/person.

Healthy life expectancy and freedom to make life choices

This highlights a strong positive correlation between healthy life expectancy and happiness scores , with a notable but less pronounced correlation with freedom to make life choices .

When individuals experience longer, healthier lives , it contributes significantly to their life satisfaction and contentment. In many cases, the tangible and immediate benefits of good health may outweigh the impact of the degree of freedom in certain life choices on an individual's happiness.

There are clear clusters formed by continents which reveal lower scores across the board for Africa, possibly due to a combination of socio-economic challenges. In contrast, continents like Europe and Asia consistently acheive high scores in these variables. This suggests that these continents have created conducive environments for both health and happiness, possibly due to robust healthcare systems, stable economies, and other societal factors.
